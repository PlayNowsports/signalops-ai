HANDOFF + EXECUTIVE CASE
Submission brief
The operating case, pushback response, rollout plan, workflow log, and demo record travel with the tool.

        

          
Executive memo
Decision: run Churn Tool weekly as a governed triage layer before forecast calls. It converts one CRM export into a validated risk queue, three churn scenarios, quarterly exposure, and named executive actions. It does not replace the forecast owner.
Expected outcome: shorter account-review preparation, earlier save-plan ownership, and an auditable record of why an account moved. Measure adoption by weekly run completion, action acceptance, false-positive review, and forecast error by tier.

          
CRO pushback response
You are right that CSM language is noisy and recovery is possible. I will not treat concern words as churn. I will keep the P50 forecast where structured deterioration and unresolved text agree; text-only flags stay in human review. We settle the disagreement by back-testing the flagged cohort against closed outcomes, tracking overrides, and measuring error by tier. Until that evidence supports a change, the medium case remains the operating commitment.
Read the full response
What is true in the critique. CSM notes are written for customer management, not statistical forecasting. They can be stale, cautious, inconsistent across CSMs, or describe an issue that has already been resolved. Tulip has also recovered difficult renewals before, so a negative note is not the same thing as a churn outcome.
Where I agree and disagree. I agree that text-only concern should not automatically move committed churn ARR. I disagree that the noise makes the text disposable. When an unresolved customer statement agrees with low utilization, critical health, a pipeline forecast, or a recorded churn reason, ignoring it would create a more optimistic—but less defensible—forecast.
How we settle it. For the next two forecast cycles, RevOps will retain every model flag, CRO/CSM override, cited evidence, and final outcome. We will measure observed churn by tier, false-positive rates for text-only versus combined signals, and forecast error by segment and stage. CSM leaders will review the five largest disagreements weekly and classify each note as current, resolved, or stale.
Forecast commitment. I am keeping the current medium/P50 case as the operating forecast. Accounts with combined structured and unresolved text evidence remain in the forecast; text-only accounts remain review items until confirmed. If back-testing shows the combined-signal tier materially overstates churn, I will lower the scenario rate at the next monthly calibration—not override individual accounts until the total looks better.

          
Operating model
  • RevOps: upload, validate, publish queue, monitor calibration.
  • CSM: confirm adoption and customer-context signals.
  • AE: own commercial save and expansion actions.
  • CRO/CCO: resolve the five highest-leverage escalations.
  • Cadence: weekly run; monthly override review; quarterly rule calibration.

  2. Days 31–60: route accepted actions, add backend AI proxy, monitor completion and error.
  3. Days 61–90: recalibrate scenario rates, publish governance owner, decide production rollout.

AI workflow log
  • Model: Claude Sonnet 4.6 via Messages API.
  • Time saved: roughly 90 minutes across schema review, rule scaffolding, and UI iteration; calculations were independently fixture-tested.
  • Suspect output caught: negated risk language could be marked active; the prompt now treats resolved and negated statements as no current risk.
  • Control: strict schema, invalid output scores zero, prompt version recorded.
  • Current weakness: cannot reliably separate stale risk from current risk when notes have no timestamp.

          
Prompts that did the work
View four prompts verbatim
1. Inspect the supplied CRM export and data dictionary. Identify load-bearing fields, definitional drift, contradictions, missingness, and checks that must block or warn before any churn forecast is produced.

2. Design a deterministic account-level churn and expansion scoring model that combines structured health, utilization, renewal fields, CSM notes, Gong highlights, and recorded churn reasons. Every score must retain source evidence, an owner, and a recommended action.

3. Build low, medium/P50, and high in-year churn ARR scenarios plus quarterly churn and expansion. State every rate and formula, reconcile account totals, and compare the scenario assumptions with historical closed-renewal outcomes.

4. Review structured-versus-unstructured conflicts. Find accounts where customer text materially changes the structured conclusion, explain the conflict in one sentence, and route a specific action to the correct owner without inventing facts.
