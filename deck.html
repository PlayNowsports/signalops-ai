Exercise 2C · GTM Point of View
Keep land-and-expand — fix the partner channel, not the segment mix
Erin Nelson
Keep the motion. Reallocate investment to partner, not segment targeting. Partner's win-rate advantage over direct collapsed (6.9x → 2.0x) while direct actually improved — the motion isn't broken; its highest-converting channel is underinvested. What would change my mind: (1) corrected partner attribution erases the channel gap, or (2) expansion ARR on landed accounts stops outpacing land ARR — either falsifies "land-and-expand works, channel is the problem."
Partner ÷ direct win-rate multiple
The five levers — what stops, starts, changes (per brief)
Marketing investment START partner co-marketing (MDF, joint case studies with DMG MORI + top SIs). HOLD flat on direct demand-gen — SME/MM inbound isn't the constraint; conversion is.
SDR / AE focus STOP planned direct-only SDR headcount growth until the partner audit lands. CHANGE AE comp to neutral or partner-favorable on sourced deals.
Partner channel START enablement audit (90 days), then co-selling SC time, references, battlecards. This is where the reallocated dollars go.
CSM coverage CHANGE nothing structurally in yr 1 — but partner-led accounts need a named CSM lane (today they inherit whoever; Ex.3's DACH risk concentration is partner-heavy).
Pricing / packaging HOLD — no packaging problem in this data, and a pricing change now would confound the read on whether partner enablement worked.
Risk matrix — where this could be wrong (judgment-based, not derived from data)
1 · Reporting artifact — Ex.1's 220-row attribution mismatch could inflate the channel signal. Know by: corrected attribution shrinks the gap.
2 · Partner-side cause — rep turnover / lost certification, not Tulip's to fix. Know by: asking partner managers directly.
3 · ENT fluke — n=59 FY26 closes is thin. Know by: 2 more quarters of ENT closes.
Sequence — 90 days vs. first year
Fix the channel, protect the motion — this is a reallocation call, not a strategy pivot.
